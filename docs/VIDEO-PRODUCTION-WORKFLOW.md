# Video Production Workflow — Step-by-Step Sequencing

## Short Answer

**Use PC for everything except filming yourself (which you won't do).
Record audio first, then build visuals around it in CapCut on PC.**

Phone is optional for one thing: if you want to post vertical Shorts/Reels,
you can export from CapCut PC and post from your phone. That's it.

---

## The Full Sequence (Do This Every Time)

### Step 1 — Write the Script (PC)
- Open a Google Doc
- Write exactly what you'll say, word for word
- Keep it conversational — short sentences, no jargon unless you explain it
- Aim for 130–150 words per minute of finished video
  - 5 min video = ~700 words
  - 10 min video = ~1,400 words

---

### Step 2 — Record the Voiceover (PC — do this BEFORE anything visual)

**Option A: Your own voice (best for trust/authenticity)**
- Plug in any USB mic or even AirPods into your PC
- Open **Audacity** (free) or just record directly in CapCut
- Read the script out loud — record the whole thing in one take if you can
- Don't stop for mistakes — just pause 2 seconds and re-say the line, cut it later
- Save as MP3

**Option B: AI voiceover (no mic needed)**
- Go to **ElevenLabs.io** (free tier = 10,000 characters/month)
- Paste your script, pick a voice, click generate
- Download the MP3
- Takes 30 seconds

**Why audio first?** Your visuals need to match what's being said. If you build slides first and then record audio, they never line up. Audio first = everything else follows it.

---

### Step 3 — Screen Record the App (PC, if that video type)

For "live quiz" style videos where you show the platform:

- Open **OBS Studio** (free, download at obsproject.com)
- Set it to "Window Capture" — select your browser window
- Hit Record (not Stream)
- Navigate through SPD Cert Prep normally — answer questions, show the domain tracker, use the AI chat
- Talk as you go OR stay silent and add voiceover in Step 2 after
- Stop recording — OBS saves an MP4 to your desktop

**OBS one-time setup (5 minutes):**
1. Download and install OBS
2. Click the + under Sources → Window Capture → pick Chrome/Firefox
3. Under Settings → Output → set Recording Path to your Desktop
4. Set resolution to 1920x1080
5. Done — just hit Start Recording every time

---

### Step 4 — Gather B-Roll / Visuals (PC, 10–15 min)

For non-screen-recording videos you need visuals to play while audio runs.

**Free stock footage sites:**
- pexels.com/videos — search "hospital", "surgeon", "medical instruments"
- pixabay.com — same searches
- Download 8–12 clips, drop them in a folder called `broll`

**Text slides (alternative to footage):**
- Open Canva (canva.com, free)
- Use a blank 1920x1080 template
- Create simple text slides: big bold keyword on dark background
- Export as PNG images — one per key point in your script

---

### Step 5 — Edit in CapCut PC (PC)

Download **CapCut for Desktop** (free at capcut.com — use the desktop version not the web version, it's faster).

**Edit sequence inside CapCut:**

1. **Import everything:** drag your voiceover MP3, screen recording MP4, and b-roll clips into the Media panel
2. **Drop voiceover on the timeline first** — this is your backbone
3. **Drop visuals on the video track above the audio** — cut them to match what's being said
4. **Add captions:**
   - Click "Text" → "Auto Captions" → CapCut transcribes your audio and adds subtitles automatically
   - Fix any mistakes, style them (white text, black outline, bottom-center)
5. **Add background music:**
   - Click "Audio" → "Music" → search "focus" or "ambient" in CapCut's built-in library (all copyright-free)
   - Drop it on a third audio track, set volume to 8–12% (just barely audible under your voice)
6. **Add intro text:** First 3 seconds — show your channel name or video title as a text overlay
7. **Add end screen placeholder:** Last 20 seconds — blank dark screen with "Subscribe for more CRCST prep" text
8. **Export:** Click Export → 1080p → H.264 → save to Desktop

Total edit time once you know CapCut: **45–90 minutes per video**

---

### Step 6 — Make the Thumbnail (PC, Canva)

- Open canva.com
- New design → YouTube Thumbnail (1280x720)
- Dark background (navy #0A1628 or dark teal)
- 3–5 word headline in bold white or amber
- One icon or simple graphic
- Export as PNG
- Do NOT use small text — thumbnails are viewed at 120px wide on mobile

---

### Step 7 — Upload to YouTube (PC or Phone)

**On PC:**
- Go to youtube.com → click the camera + icon → Upload Video
- Drop your MP4 in
- While it processes, fill in:
  - Title (include keyword in first 5 words)
  - Description (150+ words, app link in first 3 lines)
  - Tags (10–15, mix of broad and specific)
  - Thumbnail (upload your Canva PNG)
  - Add to playlist "CRCST Exam Prep 2026"
- Set visibility to "Public" or schedule it

## First Video — Fastest Path

The fastest first video to produce is the **screen recording + AI voiceover** format:

1. Write a 700-word script for "AAMI ST79 Explained in 5 Minutes" (30 min)
2. Paste into ElevenLabs, download voiceover MP3 (5 min)
3. Record screen — open SPD Cert Prep, navigate around while audio plays (15 min)
4. Drop both into CapCut, cut to sync, add auto-captions, add music (60 min)
5. Make thumbnail in Canva (15 min)
6. Upload (10 min)

**Total first video: ~2.5 hours**

Videos 2–5 go faster as the workflow becomes routine — target 90 min/video by week 4.
